# DISCOVERY: Compound Death Spiral is Primary Failure Mode

## Finding

The most probable failure path (~40-50% probability) is NOT incumbent response or technical failure — it's a **compound death spiral**:

```
Slow guide recruitment
    ↓
Poor matching quality (not enough guides for good matches)
    ↓
Low tourist satisfaction
    ↓
Low repeat booking rate
    ↓
Poor unit economics
    ↓
Can't afford guide acquisition
    ↓
Back to slow guide recruitment
```

## Why This Matters

The pitch deck frames this as "build guide density → tourists come → data moat." But the flywheel can spin **backwards** equally fast.

## Key Insight

The chicken-and-egg problem is not symmetric. Guide acquisition is HARDER than tourist acquisition because:

- Guides need to believe there's demand before they join
- Tourists need to believe there's supply before they book
- Supply (guides) is more "sticky" — a guide who's been on the platform for 6 months has reputation invested

## Implication for Building

**Guide density before tourist marketing scale** is the single most important execution principle. All marketing spend should be gated on guide density thresholds, not time from launch.
