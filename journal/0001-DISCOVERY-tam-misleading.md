# DISCOVERY: TAM is Catalog Market, Not Matching Market

## Finding

The $300B TAM cited in the pitch deck is the global tours/activities market — a catalog browsing market, NOT a matching market. These are fundamentally different businesses.

## Why This Matters

- Catalog market: Klook, GetYourGuide, Viator, Airbnb Experiences
- Matching market: What WanderLess actually competes in
- No direct competitor exists in matching, so the "TAM" is aspirational positioning, not competitive market size

## Technical Nuance

A catalog marketplace matches tourists to **products** (pre-packaged tours). WanderLess matches tourists to **people** (guides with expertise and personality). The unit economics, success metrics, and competitive dynamics are completely different.

## Implication for Building

The matching market is much smaller initially (probably $2-5B globally) but has:

- Higher margins (better unit economics)
- Stronger network effects (person-to-person, not product-to-person)
- More defensible moats (guide expertise can't be copied by adding a feature)
