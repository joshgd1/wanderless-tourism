# CONNECTION: Interest Vectors Enable Cross-City Network Effects

## Connection

The pitch deck treats geographic expansion as city-by-city with isolated network effects. But the interest-vector architecture creates a **novel cross-city network effect** that was not explicitly identified.

## The Insight

A tourist's interest profile (64-dimensional vector) is **portable across cities**. A "food adventurer" from Bangkok who visits Chiang Mai carries the same interest vector. This means:

1. Guide expertise learned in city A helps matching in city B
2. Tourist segments created in city A transfer to city B
3. Collaborative filtering benefits compound across all cities simultaneously

## Why This Is Non-Obvious

Traditional geographic marketplaces (Uber, Airbnb) have purely local network effects. Each city is its own island. WanderLess's interest-vector approach creates **supra-geographic network effects** that accelerate expansion.

## Implication for Building

The expansion strategy should:

1. Prioritize cities that attract **diverse tourist segments** (not just one type)
2. Aggregate interest data across cities to accelerate new city matching
3. Design data infrastructure to share learning across cities from day 1

## Strategic Recommendation

If true, this means:

- First city selection matters less than initially thought
- Chiang Mai + Bangkok + Penang creates compounding faster than 3x Chiang Mai
- A tourist who visits 3 WanderLess cities generates 3x the training data value
